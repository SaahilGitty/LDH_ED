Welcome from the Luton and Dunstable ED Team!

The Luton and Dunstable University Hospital’s Emergency Department operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year and provides a comprehensive emergency service for Luton, South Bedfordshire and parts of Hertfordshire – a total population of almost 400,000. Our ED is one of the busiest in the region, in 2017 we saw more than 80,000 patients, a further 35,000 patients were seen by an ‘Urgent GP’ service, which is located on site. The ED is divided into four areas:

Resuscitation

For the most sick and severely injured patients (usually arriving by ambulance), highly equipped and allows advanced management of patients.

Majors

For the majority of sick patients and provides a trolley based area for examinations and treatment.

See & Treat

For minor injuries and illnesses, and is staffed primarily by our Nurse Practitioners.

Paediatrics

In 2012 we saw and treated almost 18,000 children. We now have a facility, the Paediatric Emergency Department is staffed by children’s nurses.

Same Day Emergency Care (SDEC)

Our SDEC is located adjacent to the ED and offers modern and comfortable facilities for the arrival of sick patients, referred as emergencies by their GPs. Located within the SDEC are facilities for the rapid assessment and management of patients with ambulatory conditions. This means that patients can be started on a treatment pathway and allowed to go home quickly for continued care. The SDEC is also a reception area for further assessment of patients who have been seen initially within the ED. It is staffed 24 hours a day by experienced medical and nursing staff, including three dedicated Acute Care Physicians, who also provide Consultant cover to the Medical Short stay unit and Ambulatory Care Centre.

Induction

The Medical Education Department is committed to the on-going training, development and education of the Trust’s medical and clinical staff in line with the Trust objective to secure and develop a workforce to meet the needs of our patients and ensure our medical and dental clinical staff, have the ability, skills, knowledge and expertise to provide high quality, safe and effective services.

All new medical and dental staff MUST attend a Medical Induction programme. The induction programme focuses on the mandatory training required to work within the Trust and includes both clinical and non-clinical based training to ensure compliance before you begin your career with us. Following induction, there will be a tailored departmental induction which will introduce you to your team, ward/department and provide information relevant to the speciality you are working in. For more information regarding Medical Education and for your complete Junior Doctor Induction pack, visit the Medical Education Website.

The Foundation Programme is a two-year training programme that forms the bridge between medical school and specialty/general practice training. The Trust has 29 year one trainees and 29 year two trainees who are allocated via Essex, Bedfordshire & Hertfordshire Foundation School (EBHFS). More information is available from Kiran Maqbool.

Our General Practice Specialty Training Programme is spread across a three year period. Eighteen months is spent in General Practice and the remaining time is either at the Luton and Dunstable University Hospital, Bedford Hospital or in the community. The GP Speciality Training Directors currently provide a comprehensive training programme for 75 GP trainees across 23 rotations. Information is available from Makeda Bent and Carol Hylton.

The L&D currently provides specialty training programmes in the following areas:- Acute Care, Emergency Medicine, Anaesthetics, Intensive Care, Medicine and medical specialties, Radiology, Obstetrics & Gynaecology, Ophthalmology, Paediatrics, General Surgery and surgical specialties for approximately 200 trainees.

There are currently 14 trainees undertaking dental training three of which are vocational training posts. Trainees have placements at Luton & Dunstable, Bedford, East & North Herts & Milton Keynes Hospitals. We also run various CPD HEE EoE approved courses at our Bedford Hospital site. More information regarding the courses can be found on HEE's Website or via Intrepid Course Manager.

The L&D welcomes medical students from UCL Medical School throughout the year and by arrangement with the medical schools provides a programme of clinical placements and education to support their education. Information is available from Jane Colclough.

A programme of weekly Grand Round meetings take place at 12.30 on Tuesdays from September to July. Details of this programme can be obtained from Ruby Murad.

Staff within Medical Education administer the study leave application and approval process for all medical and dental staff on behalf of the Director of Medical Education/Clinical Tutor.

All junior medical and dental staff doctor have a designated Educational Supervisor who meets with the junior to discuss personal development plans, study leave requirements, to review progress made, to discuss any concerns, to offer support and guidance, and in addition, can offer general career and counselling advice.

Within COMET there is a large Lecture Hall with up-to-date audio-visual facilities, plus seven smaller well-equipped seminar rooms, a Multi-skills Laboratory, Simulation rooms and an IT suite. The Training & Development department can be found on the first floor. To book training facilities please email us at COMET bookings.

The COMET Library can be found on the first floor and the office is staffed during normal office hours. Visitors must identify themselves to the librarian as the library is not an open-access area of the hospital.

The main entrance to the COMET Centre is located on the ground floor of the old block of the Hospital with the Library and Training & Development on first floor. Offices are open from Monday to Friday, 08.30 – 17.00.
